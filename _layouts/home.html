More About Me

While I enjoy programming for fun and using the latest technology, I have come to terms with a few things: Software architecture, design, and user experience are of utmost importance when it comes to creating software that will be maintained for years and enjoyed by customers, thus leading to lower costs and higher profits.

That being said, I love getting my hands dirty with new technologies. This is how I learned about image recognition using OpenCV, GraphQL, and Unity3D, while bringing new tools and frameworks like Webpack and Angular 2+ into full-stack applications at BP3. I am currently learning about Data Science and its applications in Machine Learning, which I hope to be able to apply to both my workplace and personal projects.

Education

In 2014, I graduated from the top undergraduate engineering college in the United States, located in Terre Haute, Indiana, with a double major (Bachelor of Science) in Computer Science and Software Engineering.

Work

After college, I spent 3 years at BP3 Global as a Software Engineer. I usually worked in teams of 2-3 people, gathering requirements, architecting, designing, implementing, testing, releasing, and maintaining full-stack products. Occassionally, I would be assigned to solo projects (consulting for customers), and research and develop specialized applications for internal use or potential new products.

At my workplace, we practiced Agile development. Scrum was widely practiced (with 15-minute daily standups and generally 1-2 week long sprints), but we started experimenting with Kanban (with varying work in-progress limits) in 2017, which seemed to work better for projects that were driven by customer feature requests.
